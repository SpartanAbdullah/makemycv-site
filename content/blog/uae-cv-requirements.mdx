**A UAE CV requires a few details Western CVs leave out: visa status, a professional summary, and often nationality and a photo for client-facing roles.** Include your contact details, achievement-based experience, and attested education, and leave off salary history, references, and personal essays. Getting these specifics right is what clears the first recruiter filter in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

A UAE CV is not just a translated version of a Western one. The Gulf market expects certain details a US or UK CV deliberately omits — and including the wrong ones, or leaving out the right ones, is a quiet reason qualified candidates get filtered out. Here is exactly what a UAE CV requires in 2026, and what to leave off.

## What a UAE CV Must Include

### Contact details done right

Full name, a professional email, your phone with the correct country code, and your city. If you are already in the UAE, a local number signals you are on the ground and ready. Add a LinkedIn URL — UAE recruiters check it routinely.

### Visa status

This is the requirement most newcomers miss. UAE employers need to know whether hiring you means transferring an employment visa, cancelling an existing one, or sponsoring from scratch. State it in one line near the top: "UAE Employment Visa — Transferable" or "Visit Visa — Available Immediately." Without it, recruiters either guess or move on.

### A professional summary

Three sentences under the header: your role and experience, your industry and context, and a defining achievement. It frames everything below it.

### Work experience as achievements

Company, title, dates, location, and three to five achievement bullets per role — each starting with a verb and carrying a number where possible. This is the core of the CV.

### Education, with attestation noted

Degree, university, country, and dates. Because many UAE employers require **attested** qualifications for mid-to-senior roles, note it if yours is attested: "Bachelor of Commerce — University of Mumbai (Attested for UAE)."

### Skills and languages

A clean, comma-separated skills list of genuine tools and competencies (these are your ATS keywords), and languages with honest proficiency levels — which matter more in the multilingual Gulf than almost anywhere.

## What's Optional (and Context-Dependent)

### A photo

A small professional headshot is normal and frequently expected in the UAE, especially for client-facing roles in hospitality, banking, real estate, and retail. It is optional and carries none of the discrimination concerns it would in the UK or Canada. We cover the decision fully in [should you put a photo on your UAE CV](/blog/photo-on-uae-cv).

### Nationality and marital status

The UAE is genuinely split here. Some local and government-adjacent employers still expect these; many multinationals prefer you omit them. A reasonable rule: include for UAE government or local conglomerate roles, omit for European-led or multinational HQ roles.

### Date of birth

Increasingly optional and often best left off, but still seen on UAE CVs. If a specific employer or agency requests it, add it; otherwise it is safe to omit.

## What to Leave Off

- **Salary history** — never on the CV; discuss it later in the process.
- **References** — "Available on request" is unnecessary; recruiters ask when they need them.
- **Every job since 2008** — anything older than ten years becomes a single summary line.
- **Personal essays and objectives** — replace the old "objective" with a sharp professional summary.
- **Religious or political affiliations** — irrelevant and best omitted.

## Format Requirements That Are Easy to Miss

Requirements are not only about content. A UAE CV should be one to two pages, single column, in a standard font, saved as a PDF with selectable text (not an exported image). These are the rules that keep the [ATS](/blog/ats-cv-checklist-uae) able to read you. The full layout spec is in the [UAE CV format guide](/blog/cv-format-uae-2026).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What must a UAE CV include?
Contact details with the correct country code, visa status, a three-sentence professional summary, achievement-based work experience, and education with attestation noted where relevant. These are the fields UAE recruiters scan for first.

### Do I need to put my visa status on a UAE CV?
Yes — it is the requirement newcomers most often miss. State it in one line near the top, such as "UAE Employment Visa — Transferable," so recruiters know what hiring you involves.

### Should I include nationality, a photo, and date of birth?
These are optional and context-dependent. Nationality and a photo are common for local and client-facing roles but often omitted for Western-led multinationals; date of birth is increasingly left off. See [should you put a photo on your UAE CV](/blog/photo-on-uae-cv).

### What should I leave off a UAE CV?
Salary history, "references available on request," older jobs in full detail, personal objectives, and religious or political affiliations. Trimming these keeps the CV to the expected [one-to-two-page format](/blog/cv-format-uae-2026).
