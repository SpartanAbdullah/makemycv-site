**Short answer: a cover letter isn't always required for UAE jobs, but it's strongly recommended — and it's often the tie-breaker.** A single Dubai role can attract over a thousand applications within 48 hours, so recruiters look for fast reasons to shortlist. A short, specific cover letter that names the role, shows one relevant result, and states your **visa status and availability** up front can move you ahead of identical-looking CVs. Keep it under one page.

---

## When a Cover Letter Is Worth Writing

Include one when:

- **The job ad asks for it** — always follow instructions; a missing cover letter can auto-disqualify you.
- **You're applying by email or direct message** — the body of your email *is* your cover letter.
- **You're changing industry, relocating, or have a gap** — a few lines of context help the recruiter make sense of your CV.
- **It's a competitive or senior role** — when many CVs look alike, the letter is your tie-breaker.

You can usually skip a formal letter when applying through a portal that only accepts a CV, or a quick-apply button — but even then, a two-line note adds a human touch.

## What a UAE Cover Letter Must Include

UAE cover letters have two details that Western ones often leave out — and recruiters look for them early:

- **Current location** (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, or "open to both")
- **Visa status** (Employment / Golden / Visit / requires sponsorship) and **notice period / earliest joining date**

Put these in the first two or three lines. They answer the recruiter's first questions before they even open your CV.

## Structure (Keep It to 4 Short Paragraphs)

| Paragraph | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Opening** | Role you're applying for, where you saw it, and one line on why you fit |
| **Eligibility** | Current location, visa status, and availability |
| **Proof** | One or two quantified achievements that match the job's top requirement |
| **Close** | A confident line and a clear call to action (happy to interview / share references) |

## Short UAE Cover Letter Template

> Dear [Hiring Manager / Recruiter name],
>
> I'm applying for the **[Job Title]** role at **[Company]** advertised on [LinkedIn / Bayt / careers page]. With **[X years]** in **[field]** in the UAE, I believe I can [one specific outcome they need].
>
> I'm currently based in **[Dubai / Abu Dhabi]** on a **[visa status]** and available to join **[immediately / with 30 days' notice]**.
>
> In my current role at **[Company]**, I **[quantified achievement — e.g. "cut procurement costs 15% by renegotiating three supplier contracts"]**. I've attached my CV with more detail.
>
> I'd welcome the chance to discuss how I can contribute to **[Company]**. Thank you for your time.
>
> Kind regards,
> **[Your name]** · **[+971 number]** · **[email]**

## Common Cover Letter Mistakes in the UAE

1. **Writing three paragraphs of autobiography.** Recruiters skim — keep it under one page, ideally 150–200 words for an email.
2. **Leaving out visa status and availability.** These are the first things UAE recruiters check.
3. **Reusing one generic letter.** Name the company and the specific requirement, or don't bother.
4. **Letting AI write it wholesale.** Use AI to tighten wording, but keep it specific and true. (See our guide on [using ChatGPT for your CV](/blog/can-chatgpt-write-cv).)
5. **Repeating your CV word for word.** The letter should *add* context, not duplicate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do UAE employers require a cover letter?

Not always. Many applications accept a CV only. But when a job ad requests one, or when you apply by email, a short, tailored cover letter is expected — and it helps you stand out in a high-volume market.

### How long should a UAE cover letter be?

Under one page. For an email application, 150–200 words in the body is ideal. Recruiters don't have time for a long letter.

### What should a Dubai cover letter include that others don't?

Your current location, visa status, and availability — placed in the first few lines — plus one or two quantified achievements relevant to the role.

### Should I attach the cover letter or put it in the email body?

If you're applying by email, put it in the body and attach your CV. If a portal has a cover-letter field, paste it there. Only attach a separate letter when the ad asks for one.

### Can I use the same cover letter for every job?

No. Keep a template, but change the company name, the role, and the one achievement that matches each job's top requirement.
