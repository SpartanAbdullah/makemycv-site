**Short answer: a UAE nursing or healthcare CV must lead with your licensing status, not just your experience.** UAE health authorities and hospital recruiters look first for your **licence** (DHA for Dubai, DOH for Abu Dhabi, MOHAP for the other emirates — active, or in progress), your **DataFlow** primary-source verification status, and your **BLS/ACLS/PALS** certifications with issuers and expiry dates. Put those at the top, then structure clinical experience by specialty. This is what gets a healthcare CV shortlisted in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

---

> This guide covers CV structure and content. Always confirm current licensing requirements and fees on the official [DHA](https://www.dha.gov.ae/), [DOH](https://www.doh.gov.ae/), and [MOHAP](https://mohap.gov.ae/) portals before applying.

## First, Know Which Authority Licenses You

The UAE has three regulators depending on where you'll work:

| Authority | Covers | Licensing portal / exam |
|---|---|---|
| **DHA** (Dubai Health Authority) | Dubai | Sheryan (Prometric exam) |
| **DOH** (Department of Health) | Abu Dhabi | Mawared (Pearson VUE exam) |
| **MOHAP** (Ministry of Health & Prevention) | Sharjah, Ajman, UAQ, RAK, Fujairah | Mumaris+ (Prometric exam) |

All non-UAE applicants must also complete **DataFlow** primary-source verification (PSV) of their degree, licence, good-standing certificate, and experience before a licence is issued. Your CV should signal where you are in this process.

## Put Licensing at the Top of Your CV

Right after your header, add a short **Licensing & Certifications** block so a recruiter sees eligibility in seconds:

> **Licensing & Verification**
> • DHA Eligibility Letter issued (Prometric exam scheduled) · DataFlow PSV completed
> • BLS (AHA, valid to 2027) · ACLS (AHA, valid to 2027)
> • Registered Nurse — [home-country council], [registration no.]

If you're already licensed, say so plainly ("DHA-licensed Registered Nurse"). If you're mid-process, name the stage ("DataFlow verification in progress"). Recruiters plan around this.

## Recommended Structure for a UAE Healthcare CV

1. **Header** — name, target role (e.g. "Registered Nurse — ICU"), city, +971 number, email, and **visa status**.
2. **Licensing & verification** — as above (DHA/DOH/MOHAP status + DataFlow + life-support certs).
3. **Professional summary** — 3–4 lines: title, years, specialty, and one measurable strength.
4. **Clinical experience** — reverse-chronological, grouped or labelled **by specialty** (e.g. ICU, ER, Med-Surg), with patient load, unit size, and outcomes.
5. **Education** — nursing degree/diploma, institution, year.
6. **Certifications & training** — BLS/ACLS/PALS/NRP, specialty courses, with issuer and expiry.
7. **Skills & systems** — clinical skills plus any EMR/HIS you've used (e.g. Cerner, Malaffi, Salama).
8. **Languages** — English is essential; Arabic is a strong plus.

## Make Clinical Experience Recruiter-Ready

Turn duties into evidence. Every role should show scale and outcomes:

- ❌ "Responsible for patient care in ICU."
- ✅ "Managed a 6-bed ICU assignment on rotating shifts; maintained zero central-line infections over 12 months; precepted 3 new graduate nurses."

Numbers a healthcare recruiter values: bed/patient load, unit size, shift type, specialty procedures, audit or safety metrics, and EMR systems used.

## Keep It ATS-Friendly

Hospital groups screen high volumes through applicant tracking systems. Keep the CV single-column, use standard headings, save as a text-based **PDF**, and mirror keywords from the job posting (specialty, certifications, licence type). ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What licence do I need to work as a nurse in the UAE?

It depends on the emirate: **DHA** for Dubai, **DOH** for Abu Dhabi, and **MOHAP** for Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, Ras Al Khaimah, and Fujairah. Each has its own exam portal, and non-UAE applicants must complete DataFlow verification.

### What is DataFlow and should it go on my CV?

DataFlow is the UAE's mandatory primary-source verification of your credentials before licensing. Yes — noting your DataFlow status (completed or in progress) reassures recruiters you're moving toward eligibility.

### What should a UAE nursing CV lead with?

Your licensing and verification status (DHA/DOH/MOHAP + DataFlow) and life-support certifications, followed by specialty-organised clinical experience. Eligibility comes before experience for regulated healthcare roles.

### Do I need the exam passed before applying?

Not always. Many hospitals consider candidates who hold an eligibility letter or are mid-process. State exactly where you are — an offer often runs in parallel with final licensing.

### How long should a healthcare CV be in the UAE?

One to two pages. Lead with licensing and your strongest specialty experience; don't pad it to three pages.
