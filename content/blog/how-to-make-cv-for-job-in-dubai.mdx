A Dubai CV is a UAE CV with the dial turned toward Dubai's specific hiring machine: more ATS screening, more WhatsApp recruiting, more competition per posting, and recruiters who think in ecosystems — DIFC, free zones, mainland — rather than just job titles. The base rules are in our step-by-step [UAE CV guide](/blog/how-to-make-cv-for-job-in-uae); this guide covers what changes when the job is specifically in Dubai.

## Know Which Dubai You're Applying To

"Dubai" is at least four hiring markets wearing one skyline:

- **DIFC (financial centre):** banks, funds, fintech, legal. English-first, heavily regulated, ATS-screened. Compliance vocabulary matters — DFSA, KYC/AML, Basel, IFRS. If you have it, your CV should say it.
- **Free zones (JAFZA, DMCC, Dubai Internet/Media City, D3):** trading companies, logistics, tech, media. Often smaller HR teams — your CV may be read by a founder, not a recruiter. Clarity beats formality.
- **Mainland corporates:** the giants — Emirates Group, Emaar, ENOC, Majid Al Futtaim, du, e&. High application volume, strict ATS screening, structured grading. This is where parse-safety decides outcomes before humans are involved.
- **Hospitality and retail:** Jumeirah, Address, Atlantis, malls' retail groups. Photo-friendly, presentation-weighted, often hiring at volume through agencies.

One CV can serve all four — but the professional summary and keywords should shift per application. Mirroring three or four exact phrases from the job posting is the cheapest ranking improvement available.

## The Dubai-Specific Adjustments

### 1. The WhatsApp test

Dubai recruiters and agencies routinely ask for your CV over WhatsApp. That means: a single PDF, under a couple of MB, with a filename that works in a chat thread — `Omar-Hassan-CV-2026.pdf`, not `final_v7 (3).pdf`. Your CV's first impression may literally be a chat bubble preview.

### 2. Availability and notice period, stated

Dubai moves fast; agencies frequently need candidates who can start within the month. If you're on a visit visa and available immediately, that line near the top is an advantage — say it plainly. If you're employed, state your notice period. Recruiters shortlist around these logistics.

### 3. Salary expectations — leave them off the CV

Dubai postings often ask for expected salary in the application form. Answer there, not on the CV document itself, where a number can only filter you out.

### 4. Driving licence

For sales, logistics, real estate, and field-service roles in Dubai, "Valid UAE Driving Licence" is a screening keyword. It has a dedicated optional field in [the builder](https://app.makemycv.ae) for exactly this reason.

### 5. Dubai vocabulary

RERA certification for real estate. HACCP for F&B. DHA/MOH licensing for healthcare. Tasheel/GDRFA familiarity for PRO roles. Each industry has two or three Dubai-specific tokens that ATS filters and human screeners both look for — your job is to include the ones you legitimately hold.
