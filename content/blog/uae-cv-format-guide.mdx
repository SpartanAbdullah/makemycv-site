## Why UAE CVs Are Different

The UAE job market is unlike any other. With over 200 nationalities competing
for roles at the same companies, your CV needs to work harder than anywhere else.

Recruiters at major UAE employers — ENOC, Emaar, ADNOC, Emirates Group —
receive hundreds of applications per role. Most are filtered automatically
before a human ever reads them.

## The 5 Most Common UAE CV Mistakes

**1. Wrong length** — UAE recruiters expect 1-2 pages maximum.
Three-page CVs signal poor communication skills.

**2. Missing personal details** — Unlike Western CVs, UAE CVs
typically include nationality, visa status, and sometimes a
professional photo.

**3. Generic objective statements** — "Seeking a challenging role
in a dynamic environment" tells a recruiter nothing. Replace with
a 2-line professional summary tied to the specific role.

**4. No keyword alignment** — ATS systems scan for exact keywords
from the job description. If the job says "procurement management"
and your CV says "purchasing", you may not match.

**5. Incorrect date format** — Use "January 2024 – Present" not
"01/2024 – Now".

## The Correct UAE CV Structure

Follow this exact order for maximum ATS compatibility:

1. **Full Name** — Large, bold, at the top
2. **Professional Title** — Your current or target job title
3. **Contact Details** — Phone, email, LinkedIn, location (city only)
4. **Professional Summary** — 2-3 lines, keyword-rich
5. **Work Experience** — Reverse chronological, with bullet points
6. **Education** — Most recent first
7. **Skills** — Hard skills only, match job descriptions
8. **Languages** — With proficiency levels
9. **Certifications** — Relevant and recent only

## ATS Formatting Rules

Keep your formatting ATS-safe:

- Use standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman
- No tables, columns, or text boxes in the main content
- No headers/footers for contact information
- Save as PDF for applications (unless DOCX is requested)
- Use standard section headings (not creative alternatives)
