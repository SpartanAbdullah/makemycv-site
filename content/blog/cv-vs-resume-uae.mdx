If you have applied for jobs across different countries, the CV-versus-resume question gets confusing fast — the same two words mean different things depending on where you are. For UAE job seekers, here is the clear answer: what the difference actually is, and which one to send in Dubai or Abu Dhabi.

## Are They the Same in the UAE?

In everyday UAE usage, **"CV" and "resume" are used interchangeably**, and the document people send is called a CV. So when a Dubai job posting asks for your "CV," it wants what an American would call a resume: a concise, one-to-two-page summary of your experience tailored to the role — not a long academic dossier.

This matters because the words mean different things elsewhere, and that is the source of the confusion.

## The Real Difference (and Why It Confuses People)

The distinction depends entirely on region.

In the **US and Canada**, a *resume* is a short, targeted, one-to-two-page marketing document, while a *CV* (curriculum vitae) is a long, exhaustive academic record listing every publication, grant, and position — used mainly in academia and research.

In the **UK, Europe, and much of the Commonwealth**, "CV" is simply the standard word for a job application document, and it is short and targeted — essentially what Americans call a resume.

The **UAE follows the UK/Commonwealth convention**: "CV" is the everyday term, and it should be short and targeted. So an American arriving in Dubai should send their *resume* and simply call it a CV — not their long academic CV, which would be far too long for a UAE recruiter doing a seven-second scan.

## So Which Do You Send for a UAE Job?

Send a **concise, tailored CV of one to two pages.** Whatever you call it, the UAE expectation is the short, targeted document:

- One to two pages, single column, ATS-friendly
- A three-line professional summary, not a long personal statement
- Achievement-focused work experience
- The UAE-specific fields — visa status, optional photo, attestation

If you arrive with a six-page US-style academic CV, trim it hard to the UAE format. The [UAE CV format guide](/blog/cv-format-uae-2026) and the [UAE CV requirements](/blog/uae-cv-requirements) cover exactly what to keep.

## Is a CV "Accepted" in Dubai?

Yes — a CV is the standard and expected document for virtually every private-sector role in Dubai and across the UAE. There is no separate "resume" format you need instead. The only thing to get right is the *style*: short, targeted, and UAE-aware, rather than a long academic record.

## Which Is More Professional?

Neither is inherently more professional — they are regional names for the same idea. What reads as professional in the UAE is a clean, concise, well-structured CV that respects the recruiter's time and passes the [ATS](/blog/ats-cv-checklist-uae). A tidy two-page CV beats an exhaustive multi-page document every time in this market.
