**With no work experience, lead your UAE CV with Education, then use internships, academic projects, and university activities as your "experience" — each written as a measurable result, not a description.** Keep it to **one page**, put your visa status and availability at the top, and replace the senior-style Professional Summary with a focused 3-line Career Objective. UAE recruiters do hire freshers — they just need to see potential, relevant skills, and honest eligibility fast.

---

## Fresh-Graduate CV Structure (In Order)

For a first job in Dubai or Abu Dhabi, order your sections like this:

1. **Personal details** — full name, target role, Dubai/UAE location, +971 number, professional email, LinkedIn, and **visa status** (see below).
2. **Career objective** — 2–3 lines naming your target role, sector, and one strong signal.
3. **Education** — degree, university, graduation year, and grade/CGPA if strong.
4. **Internships** — treat these as real jobs, with quantified bullets.
5. **Academic / personal projects** — especially anything with tools, data, or an outcome.
6. **Skills** — technical tools and software, plus languages.
7. **Certifications & achievements** — courses, awards, scholarships, volunteering.

For a fresher, **one page is the rule.** A two-page CV with little experience signals you can't prioritise.

## Write a Career Objective, Not a Professional Summary

Experienced professionals open with a Professional Summary of past results. As a fresher, you open with a **Career Objective** — short, specific, and forward-looking. Name the role, the sector, and one differentiator: a strong GPA, a recognisable internship, a certification, or a language.

> "Recent Finance graduate (BBA, 3.6 GPA) seeking an analyst role in Dubai. Completed a 3-month internship at a DIFC advisory firm building Excel models for client portfolios. Fluent in English and Arabic."

That's concrete and honest — no empty "hardworking team player looking for opportunities." More wording patterns (for when you *do* have experience) are in our [professional summary examples](/blog/professional-summary-examples-uae-cv).

## Turn Internships and Projects Into Results

With no full-time history, your internships and projects *are* your experience. Give every bullet at least one number:

- ❌ "Helped the marketing team with social media."
- ✅ "Scheduled 40+ posts over an 8-week internship; grew the brand's Instagram following 12%."
- ❌ "Did a final-year project on data analysis."
- ✅ "Built a Python model on a 5,000-row dataset to predict customer churn; presented findings to a panel of 4 faculty."

Numbers — dataset size, weeks, AED handled, people reached, panel size — make an inexperienced CV credible. For weak-vs-strong bullet rewrites across six sectors, see the [UAE CV examples page](/cv-examples-uae).

## Handle Visa Status Honestly

This is where many fresher CVs go wrong. UAE employers *do* sponsor freshers — especially in tech, hospitality, and healthcare — but they need to know your situation up front. State it plainly in your personal details:

- "On Visit Visa — open to sponsorship"
- "Student/Family Visa — sponsorship required"
- "Golden Visa" or "UAE National" if applicable

Hiding it wastes everyone's time; stating it clearly signals honesty and lets the right employers proceed.

## What UAE Employers Look For in a Fresher

Because they can't judge you on career results, UAE recruiters weigh:

- **Degree relevance** and university recognition
- **Internship quality** — especially at recognisable UAE or international employers
- **Demonstrable skills** — name the actual tools and software
- **Languages** — English is the baseline; Arabic is a strong plus; Hindi, Urdu, and Tagalog are valued too
- **Visa status** and availability

And before any human weighs anything, your CV usually has to survive automated screening — most established UAE employers [run CVs through ATS software first](/blog/does-dubai-use-ats), so keep the layout single-column with standard headings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I write a CV for a UAE job with no experience?

Lead with Education, then present internships, academic projects, and university activities as quantified "experience." Add a short Career Objective, list your skills and languages, keep it to one page, and state your visa status clearly near the top.

### What do I put on a fresher CV instead of work experience?

Internships, academic and personal projects, part-time or volunteer work, university leadership, competitions, and certifications — each written as a measurable result rather than a description of duties.

### How long should a fresh-graduate CV be in the UAE?

One page. Recruiters spend only seconds on a first-pass fresher scan, and a two-page CV with little experience works against you.

### Will UAE companies sponsor a fresh graduate's visa?

Many will, particularly in tech, hospitality, and healthcare. State your current visa status and availability clearly so employers who can sponsor know where you stand from the first read.

### Do I need a photo on a fresher CV?

It's optional — the same rule applies as for any UAE CV. For most corporate and technical roles you can leave it off; it's more common in client-facing sectors. See [Should Your CV Have a Photo in the UAE?](/blog/should-cv-have-photo-uae)
