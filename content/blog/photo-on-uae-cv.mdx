**Yes, you can put a photo on a UAE CV — it is normal and often expected, unlike in the US or UK.** A small, professional headshot helps for client-facing roles in hospitality, banking, real estate, and retail, but it stays optional and is best left off for many technical roles or Western-headquartered multinationals.

It is one of the most common questions UAE job seekers ask, especially anyone arriving from the US, UK, or Canada where the advice is a firm "never": should your CV have a photo? In the UAE, the answer is different from the West — and getting it right is worth understanding before you apply.

## The Short Answer

In the UAE, including a small professional photo on your CV is **normal and widely accepted** — and in some industries, quietly expected. This is the opposite of US, UK, and Canadian convention, where photos are discouraged or even screened out for anti-discrimination reasons. The Gulf market simply operates differently, and applying Western "no photo ever" advice here can make your CV look out of step with local norms.

That said, a photo is optional. A strong CV without one is not at a disadvantage in most roles. The decision comes down to your industry and how the photo is done.

## When a Photo Helps

A professional headshot tends to help in **client-facing and presentation-led roles**:

- Hospitality, tourism, and aviation
- Real estate and property
- Banking and customer-facing finance
- Retail, luxury, and front-of-house
- Sales and business development
- Reception, PR, and events

In these fields, employers are accustomed to seeing a photo and its absence can read as an oversight rather than a principled choice.

## When to Leave It Off

A photo adds little — and occasionally hurts — in a few situations:

- **Multinationals with Western HQs.** Many global companies route UAE hiring through HR systems and policies that prefer no photo. When the employer is clearly a Western-headquartered multinational, omitting it is the safer call.
- **Technical and back-office roles.** Engineering, development, data, accounting, and similar roles are judged on skills; a photo is neutral at best.
- **If you only have a casual snapshot.** A poor photo is worse than none. A cropped holiday picture, a selfie, or a low-resolution image undercuts the professionalism the photo is meant to convey.

## How to Do It Right (If You Include One)

If you add a photo, treat it as part of your professional brand:

- **Professional headshot only.** Plain background, good lighting, business attire appropriate to your field.
- **Neutral, approachable expression.** A natural look — not a passport-style stare, not a party crop.
- **Small and top-aligned.** A modest size in the top corner of the header. It supports your CV; it does not dominate it.
- **High resolution, correctly placed.** It must stay crisp in the final PDF and never overlap your text.

One technical caution: a photo must not interfere with how an [ATS](/blog/ats-cv-checklist-uae) reads your CV. Some builders place images in ways that break text parsing. Keep the photo in a clean header slot and ensure all your actual content remains selectable text, not part of an image.

## The Practical Rule

If you are in a client-facing field and have a clean, professional headshot, include it. If you are in a technical role, applying to a Western multinational, or do not have a proper photo, leave it off — your experience carries the CV either way. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should a CV have a photo in the UAE?
Usually optional, often expected. A professional headshot is widely accepted in the UAE and quietly expected in client-facing fields, but a strong CV without one is not at a disadvantage in most technical or back-office roles.

### Where should the photo go on a UAE CV?
In a clean header slot, top-aligned and modestly sized, so it supports the CV without dominating it. Keep all your actual text selectable so the image does not interfere with [ATS parsing](/blog/ats-cv-checklist-uae).

### Will a photo hurt my CV with applicant tracking systems?
Only if it is placed badly. A photo embedded so that it turns your text into an image can break parsing; a photo in a clean header, with selectable text alongside it, does not.

### Do Dubai and Abu Dhabi differ on CV photos?
Neither emirate requires a photo. The bigger differences between them are tone and language — see [Dubai CV vs Abu Dhabi CV](/blog/dubai-cv-vs-abu-dhabi-cv).
