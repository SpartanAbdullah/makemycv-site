**Short answer: to change careers in the UAE, rebuild your CV around transferable skills and results — not your old job titles.** Dubai recruiters shortlist on relevance, so lead with a summary that names your target role, use a skills-forward layout that surfaces the abilities the new field needs, and translate every past achievement into language that industry understands. Done well, a career switch reads as focused and low-risk instead of scattered.

---

## Why a Normal CV Fails a Career Changer

A standard reverse-chronological CV puts your *most recent job title* first — which, for a career changer, is the thing that *doesn't* match the new role. The recruiter sees "Retail Store Manager" at the top and screens you out before reading that you ran budgets, hired teams, and analysed sales data. The fix is to reorganise so your **relevant skills lead** and your old title supports them.

## Use a Skills-Forward (Combination) Format

For a career change, a combination format works best in the UAE — it keeps the ATS-friendly chronological history recruiters expect, but adds a skills section up top:

1. **Header** — name, **target job title**, city, +971 number, email, visa status.
2. **Professional summary** — 3–4 lines that state the switch openly and frame it as a strength.
3. **Core / transferable skills** — 6–10 skills the *new* role needs, that you genuinely have.
4. **Relevant experience** — reframed achievements from past roles, mapped to the new field.
5. **Full work history** — reverse-chronological (kept, but concise).
6. **Education, certifications, courses** — especially anything you've done to retrain.

## Write a Summary That Owns the Switch

Don't hide the change — frame it. Name where you're coming from, where you're going, and why you'll succeed:

> *"Operations professional moving into project management. Six years running retail operations in Dubai — managing AED 2M budgets, 20-person teams, and supplier contracts. PMP-certified in 2026. Now applying that planning, stakeholder, and delivery experience to project delivery roles."*

That reads as deliberate, not lost.

## Translate Your Experience Into the New Field's Language

The same work can be described in the vocabulary of your target industry. Map it deliberately:

| You did (old field) | Reframe for the new field |
|---|---|
| Ran a store rota and hit sales targets | Managed resourcing and delivered against KPIs and budgets |
| Handled customer complaints | Stakeholder management and conflict resolution |
| Built a supplier spreadsheet | Data analysis and reporting |

Mirror the exact keywords from the job description so both recruiters and the [ATS](/blog/does-dubai-use-ats) recognise the match.

## Close the Credibility Gap

Switching fields raises one recruiter question: *can they actually do the new job?* Answer it with proof:

- **A relevant certification or course** (name it — PMP, CIPD, Google/AWS, a bootcamp).
- **A side project, freelance gig, or volunteer role** in the new field.
- **A transferable win** described in the new field's terms.

Even one concrete signal turns "career changer" into "credible candidate."

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I write a CV when changing careers in the UAE?

Use a skills-forward (combination) format: a target-role summary, a transferable-skills section up top, then reframed achievements and your full history. Lead with what the new role needs, not your old job title.

### Should I mention that I'm changing careers?

Yes — own it in your summary. A clear, confident line ("moving into [field] after [X] years in [field]") reads as focused. An unexplained switch looks confusing.

### What format is best for a career-change CV?

A combination format — it keeps the chronological history UAE recruiters and ATS expect while promoting your transferable skills to the top.

### How do I convince a Dubai recruiter to take a chance on me?

Show proof: a relevant certification, a side project or freelance work in the new field, and achievements translated into that industry's language. Concrete evidence beats enthusiasm.

### Will an ATS reject a career-change CV?

Not if it's keyword-matched. Mirror the target job description's terms in your skills and experience so the ATS scores you as relevant. Keep the layout single-column and standard.
