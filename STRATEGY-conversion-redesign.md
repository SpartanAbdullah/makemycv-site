# MakeMyCV marketing site — conversion & presentation strategy

**Date:** 2026-06-15 · Living doc. Goal: make `www.makemycv.ae` a high-converting landing experience where UAE job seekers see the value *loud and clear*, in our honest brand.

---

## 1. Competitor teardown — CVToolsPro (cvtoolspro.com)

**What they do well (worth adapting):**
- **Outcome headline:** "Create a **perfect CV** in minutes" — promises a result, not a feature. One coloured word for emphasis.
- **Instant proof in the hero:** a big **Before / After** CV visual — messy version (red "Informal selfie", "No keyword alignment" flags) vs polished version (green ticks: "Improve clarity instantly", "Discover missing skills"). The transformation *is* the pitch; you get it in 2 seconds.
- **Dual entry CTAs:** "Create my CV" + "Improve my CV" — captures both "starting fresh" and "I already have one".
- **Premium, airy aesthetic:** light gradient, soft sparkles, generous whitespace, rounded cards, friendly orange/blue/purple. Feels modern and trustworthy.
- **Multi-market:** language switcher; recruiter-approved framing.

**Where they're weak (our opening):**
- **Subscription (~€29.99/mo) with real billing complaints** — Trustpilot reviews repeatedly flag *automatic billing / surprise charges*. This is the category's biggest trust wound.
- **Generic-global, not UAE** — no emirate/visa/nationality nuance.
- **AI-enhancement claims that over-promise** — "transform everyday photos into corporate style", "discover missing skills for your role". This is exactly the kind of embellishment our honest brand refuses. We win by being the truthful one.

## 2. Category pain points (UAE job seekers + resume-builder users)

Reddit itself blocks automated access, so this draws on accessible MENA career sources + well-documented category patterns + review sites:

1. **Bait-and-switch paywall (the #1 complaint).** "Free to build" → spend 2–3 hours → must pay/subscribe to download. resume.io, Zety, Resume Genius all do it. Users feel tricked.
2. **Surprise recurring charges / hard cancellation** (incl. CVToolsPro reviews).
3. **AI-generated CVs now backfire** — ~67% of hiring managers can spot AI; for ~41% it's a *negative* impression. Over-polished, generic copy reads as fake.
4. **The application black hole** — ~84% of MENA job seekers feel ignored; they don't know *why* they're not getting callbacks. They want a clear diagnosis.
5. **Generic templates that don't fit the UAE** — confusion over photo, visa status, nationality, what to include/leave off.
6. **Over-complication / "where do I even start / where do I go?"** — too many choices, unclear next step.
7. **Buzzword bloat** — "leadership / expert / specialised" mean nothing to recruiters; people want concrete, true impact.

## 3. Our site today — honest audit

**Strengths (keep & amplify):** genuinely free (no trap), UAE-native fields, **JD Match** (nobody else has the heatmap), honest AI (declines rather than invents), privacy (CV stays in browser). These are *exactly* the antidotes to the pain points above — but they're under-sold visually.

**Gaps (Abdullah's concern is right):**
- Hero leads with **copy, not proof** — no instant "here's the value" demo/visual like CVToolsPro's Before/After.
- The strongest differentiators (free-no-trap, UAE-only, honest AI) aren't framed as a **loud, single promise** at the top.
- **Thin social proof** (one placeholder testimonial; stats with light sourcing).
- **Navigation is busy** (Home · JD Match · Templates · Pricing · Blog · About · Support · Contact + 2 buttons) — dilutes the primary action.
- **No memorable brand moment** — flat footer; nothing that says "premium product".
- Presentation rhythm (type scale, section transitions, consistent visuals) can feel more polished.

## 4. Positioning — what we say loud and clear

> **The only CV builder built for the UAE — genuinely free, honestly AI-assisted.**

Lead with the three things no competitor can truthfully claim together:
1. **UAE-native** (emirate, visa, nationality, attestation, GCC-aware).
2. **Free with no bait-and-switch** — build *and* download, no card, no subscription. Directly disarms the #1 fear.
3. **Honest AI** — helps you tell the truth better; declines rather than invents (and AI-stuffing now backfires with recruiters — we're the safe choice).

Plus the hook competitors don't have: **JD Match** — "see how your CV matches the job before you apply."

## 5. Proposed home architecture (section by section)

1. **Hero** — outcome headline + **instant proof visual** (the JD-Match heatmap *or* a UAE Before/After), one dominant CTA ("Build my CV — Free"), secondary "See JD Match", and a trust strip: *Free to download · No card · Private · UAE-built*.
2. **"No bait-and-switch" trust banner** — a single confident line answering the category's #1 fear: *"Free to build AND download. No watermark, no card, no subscription. Tips optional."*
3. **JD Match showcase** (already built) — the differentiator, with the heatmap.
4. **Honest-by-design + privacy** (already built).
5. **Why UAE job seekers fail / how we fix it** — reframed (already de-mythed), tied to "get found and ranked".
6. **Built-for-UAE** — emirate/visa/nationality/attestation, UAE examples.
7. **How it works** — build/import → match → tailor & download (already updated).
8. **Templates** preview.
9. **Proof** — honest testimonials (real or clearly-labelled), UAE companies users applied to.
10. **Pricing = free + optional tip** (already reframed).
11. **Final CTA**.
12. **Footer with a giant `MAKEMYCV` wordmark** (the aleads.co touch) — a full-width, oversized brand statement that makes the site feel premium and memorable.

## 6. Presentation upgrades (the "style" fix)

- **Type scale & rhythm:** slightly larger, tighter hero; consistent section heading sizes; more confident whitespace.
- **One primary CTA colour** everywhere (brand blue), ghost for secondary — stop competing buttons.
- **Consistent section motifs:** alternate light/dark with intentional rhythm; reuse the emerald/amber heatmap accent as a through-line.
- **Trust micro-proof** repeated lightly (no card · private · UAE) near CTAs.
- **Giant footer wordmark** — `MAKEMYCV` spanning the footer, subtle gradient/outline, with the tagline + tip link. This is the aleads.co-style signature moment.
- **Simplify nav** to the essentials; move secondary items into the footer.
- **Mobile-first**: every hero/visual must shine at ~380px (our audience is largely on phones).

## 7. Guardrails (unchanged)

No fear myths, no pass/fail, market only what's live, honest AI framing, keep SEO and improve it, reuse the existing design system (evolve, don't replace).
